# AARRR — Pirate Metrics

> **Category:** Analytics / Growth | **When to use:** Diagnosing where your product is leaking users

---

## The Framework

AARRR maps the user lifecycle into 5 stages. At each stage, users either progress or drop off. Your job as a PM is to find where the biggest leaks are.

| Stage | Question | Example Metric |
|---|---|---|
| **Acquisition** | How do users find you? | Organic search, referrals, paid |
| **Activation** | Do they have a great first experience? | "Aha moment" completion rate |
| **Retention** | Do they come back? | Day 7, Day 30 retention |
| **Referral** | Do they tell others? | NPS, viral coefficient |
| **Revenue** | Do they pay? | ARPU, conversion rate, LTV |

---

## Applied Example — Student Internship Marketplace

**Acquisition:** Students discover the platform via university notice boards, WhatsApp groups, Instagram → measure: weekly new signups by channel

**Activation:** A student's "aha moment" is their first completed application → measure: % of signups who complete one application within 7 days

**Retention:** Students return weekly to check application status and browse new listings → measure: Week-4 retention rate

**Referral:** Students tell coursemates → measure: % of signups from referral links

**Revenue:** Employers pay to post listings or access candidate profiles → measure: employer conversion rate from free trial

**Where to focus first:** Activation. If students sign up but never complete an application, every other metric is meaningless. Find and fix the drop-off point between signup and first application.

---

## PM Lesson

> Don't start by optimising acquisition. Start by finding your biggest leak. If your activation rate is 15%, doubling your acquisition budget just doubles the number of users you fail to activate. Fix the funnel before you fill it.
