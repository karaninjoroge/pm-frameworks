# Jobs-to-be-Done (JTBD)

> **Category:** Product Discovery | **When to use:** Understanding why users really buy/use your product

---

## The Core Idea

Users don't buy products — they hire them to get a job done. The "job" is the underlying progress they're trying to make in their life, not the feature they say they want.

**Classic example:** People don't want a drill. They want a hole. They don't want a hole. They want a shelf. They don't want a shelf. They want their room to feel organised and their partner to feel happy.

The JTBD framework forces you to ask: *what progress is the user actually trying to make?*

---

## The JTBD Statement Format

> "When I [situation], I want to [motivation / job], so I can [expected outcome]."

Or the more complete version:

> "When I [situation], I struggle to [obstacle], which makes me feel [emotional response]. I want [job to be done] so I can [desired outcome] — and I'll know I've succeeded when [success signal]."

---

## Three Types of Jobs

| Type | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Functional** | The practical task being done | Send money to family in the village |
| **Emotional** | How the user wants to feel | Feel like a responsible provider |
| **Social** | How the user wants to be perceived | Be seen as someone who looks after their family |

The best products address all three. M-Pesa does: functional (transfer money), emotional (relief that family has money), social (respected as the family member who sends support).

---

## Applied Example — Hospital Queue Management

**Surface-level request:** "I want an app to check in for my appointment."

**JTBD analysis:**

> "When I have a hospital appointment, I struggle to know how long I will actually wait — which makes me anxious and unable to plan my day. I want to manage my time around my health needs so I can remain productive and still get care — and I'll know it's working when I can leave home at a predictable time and arrive without rushing."

**What this reveals for product decisions:**
- The primary job is not "digital check-in" — it's **time certainty**
- SMS with estimated wait time may solve the job better than a complex app
- "Queue position number" is less valuable than "estimated time until called"
- The product must work for someone 2 hours away (Joseph persona) — who needs to know before they leave, not when they arrive

---

## PM Lesson

> When users ask for a specific feature, they are describing a solution they've imagined. Your job is to understand the problem they're solving — then find the best solution, which may or may not be what they asked for. JTBD is the tool that gets you from their solution request to their actual need.
