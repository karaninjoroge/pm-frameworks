# STAR Interview Framework

**Category:** Interviews
**Best For:** Structuring behavioral interview answers for PM roles

---

## What It Is

STAR is a storytelling framework for answering behavioral interview questions:

- **Situation:** Set the scene. Brief context.
- **Task:** Your specific responsibility. What were you expected to do?
- **Action:** What YOU specifically did. The core of your answer.
- **Result:** What happened? What did you learn?

---

## Why It Works

Behavioral questions follow the premise: past behavior predicts future behavior. The interviewer is not asking about the situation — they are asking about your judgment and actions within it. STAR forces you to get to the action quickly and spend the most time there.

---

## Time Allocation

For a 2-minute answer:
- Situation: 15 seconds
- Task: 15 seconds
- Action: 90 seconds ← This is where you demonstrate PM thinking
- Result: 20 seconds

Most candidates spend 60 seconds on situation and 60 seconds on result. Interviewers want to hear the action.

---

## The "I" Rule

Always say "I" in the Action section, not "we." The interviewer knows you worked with a team. They are asking what YOU specifically did. "We decided to prioritize X" tells them nothing. "I analyzed the research and made the case to the team that X should be priority because Y" tells them how you think.

---

## Converting Non-PM Experience

You do not need a PM job title to have PM stories.

| Experience | PM Question It Answers |
|---|---|
| Led a group project | "Tell me about a time you led without authority" |
| Had to figure out an unclear assignment | "Tell me about a time you dealt with ambiguity" |
| Research project where you had to change direction | "Tell me about a time you used data to change your mind" |
| Conflict with a teammate | "Tell me about a time you dealt with conflict" |
| Project that failed | "Tell me about a failure and what you learned" |

---

## Common PM Behavioral Questions

**Leadership:**
- Tell me about a time you led a project without formal authority
- Describe a time you had to influence stakeholders who disagreed with you

**Judgment:**
- Tell me about a time you had to make a decision with incomplete information
- Describe a time you had to say no to a stakeholder

**Customer Focus:**
- Tell me about a time you advocated for the user against internal pressure
- Describe a product decision you made based on user research

**Execution:**
- Tell me about a time you had to deliver under significant time pressure
- Describe a time a project you led failed to meet its goals

**Learning:**
- Tell me about your biggest professional failure and what you learned
- Describe a time you changed your mind based on new evidence
