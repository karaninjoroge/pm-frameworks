# AARRR — Pirate Metrics

**Category:** Metrics / Growth
**Best For:** Understanding where a product is losing users across the full lifecycle

---

## What It Is

AARRR (nicknamed "Pirate Metrics" for the sound) is a framework developed by Dave McClure that maps the full user lifecycle into five stages:

- **Acquisition:** How do users discover and arrive at your product?
- **Activation:** Do users have a good first experience? Do they "get it"?
- **Retention:** Do users come back?
- **Revenue:** Do users pay (directly or indirectly)?
- **Referral:** Do users tell others?

---

## Why It Matters

Most teams over-invest in Acquisition and under-invest in everything else. AARRR forces you to measure all five stages and identify where the real problem is. A leaky bucket (poor Retention) cannot be fixed by pouring in more water (more Acquisition). Fix the leak first.

---

## Applying AARRR to Different Products

**Hospital Queue Platform:**
- Acquisition: Patients learn about SMS registration (word of mouth, nurse instruction)
- Activation: Patient sends first SMS and receives a queue number successfully
- Retention: Patient uses SMS registration on their next visit
- Revenue: Facility renews subscription (B2B model)
- Referral: Patient tells family members; facility recommends to county health network

**Student Internship Marketplace:**
- Acquisition: Student finds platform via university or social media
- Activation: Student completes profile and applies for first internship
- Retention: Student uses platform for next internship search
- Revenue: Employer pays listing or placement fee
- Referral: Employer refers other SMEs; student refers classmates

---

## How to Use It

1. Define the specific metric for each stage in your product context
2. Measure conversion rate between each stage (where are you losing people?)
3. Identify the biggest drop-off — that is your highest-leverage problem
4. Fix the biggest leak before investing in Acquisition
