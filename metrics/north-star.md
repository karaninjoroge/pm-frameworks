# North Star Metric

**Category:** Metrics
**Best For:** Aligning a product team around a single measure of user value

---

## What It Is

A North Star Metric (NSM) is the single metric that best captures the core value your product delivers to users. It sits between vanity metrics (signups, page views) and business outcomes (revenue, profit). When the north star grows, the product is creating more value. When it stalls or falls, something is wrong regardless of what the business metrics show.

---

## The Key Criterion

A north star must measure **value delivered to users**, not value captured by the business.

| Bad North Star | Good North Star | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Monthly Revenue | Nights booked (Airbnb) | Revenue can grow while user value falls |
| Registered Users | Songs listened to per day (Spotify) | Registrations don't mean anyone got value |
| App Downloads | Messages sent (WhatsApp) | Downloads are one-time; messages are recurrent value |
| Patients Registered | Patients served who registered via SMS | Registration without service delivery is not value |

---

## How to Choose Your North Star

**Step 1 — Define what "working" looks like for your user**
What is the moment of genuine value delivery? The first time Airbnb worked was the first night a host successfully hosted a guest and got paid. The first time the queue platform worked was the first patient who used an SMS update to leave and come back and was seen.

**Step 2 — Find the metric that captures that moment at scale**
It should be measurable, recurring (not a one-time event), and directly tied to user behavior.

**Step 3 — Test it against the alignment criterion**
If the metric grows but users are not actually better off, it's the wrong metric. If it grows only when users are genuinely better off, it's the right one.

---

## Input Metrics

A north star is driven by 3–5 input metrics the team can directly influence. Example:

**North Star:** Patients served per day who registered via SMS

**Inputs:**
- % of patients who register via SMS (acquisition into system)
- % of registered patients who return when called (reduces abandonment)
- Staff dashboard daily active use (operational reliability)
- SMS delivery rate (infrastructure quality)

---

## Common Mistakes

**Choosing a business metric:** Revenue is a consequence of value, not a measure of it. Teams optimizing for revenue often find ways to grow it while eroding user trust.

**Choosing a vanity metric:** Sign-ups, downloads, page views — these measure attention, not value. They can grow while the product fails.

**Changing it too often:** The north star needs stability to be useful. If it changes every quarter, teams cannot build intuition around it.
