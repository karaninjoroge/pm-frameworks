# STAR Interview Framework

> **Category:** Career | **When to use:** Every PM behavioural interview question

---

## What Is STAR?

STAR is a storytelling structure that turns experiences into compelling interview answers.

| Letter | Stands for | What to cover |
|---|---|---|
| **S** | Situation | Context — where, when, what was happening |
| **T** | Task | What was your specific responsibility |
| **A** | Action | What YOU specifically did (not "we") |
| **R** | Result | Measurable outcome + what you learned |

The best PM answers add an **L** — **Learning** — making it STAR+L.

---

## STAR+L Template

**S — Situation (1–2 sentences):**
Set the scene. Give just enough context that the listener understands the stakes.

**T — Task (1 sentence):**
What was YOUR specific responsibility in this situation?

**A — Action (3–5 sentences — the heart of the answer):**
What did YOU specifically do? Use "I" not "we". Be specific about your reasoning, not just your actions.

**R — Result (1–2 sentences):**
What was the measurable outcome? If you don't have hard numbers, use directional results.

**L — Learning (1 sentence):**
What did you take away? This signals self-awareness — a critical PM trait.

---

## Common PM Interview Questions Mapped to STAR

| Question | What they're really assessing |
|---|---|
| "Tell me about a time you had to make a decision with incomplete data" | Comfort with ambiguity, analytical thinking |
| "Tell me about a product you improved" | Product sense, initiative |
| "Tell me about a conflict with an engineer or designer" | Collaboration, communication |
| "Tell me about a time you failed" | Self-awareness, resilience |
| "Tell me about a time you used data to influence a decision" | Analytical skills |
| "Tell me about a time you had to say no to a stakeholder" | Prioritisation, communication |

---

## PM Lesson

> The best STAR answers are specific to the point of being almost uncomfortable. "I worked with the team to improve the product" tells the interviewer nothing. "I interviewed 8 patients, identified that the core problem was time uncertainty (not app access), and proposed we build SMS reminders before the full app — which reduced no-shows by 15% in the pilot" — that tells them everything.
